As we come to the table, let us remember who we were.  
We were sinners, helpless and unworthy before a holy God.    
We had nothing to offer that could remove our guilt or earn His favor.    

But God demonstrated His love toward us: while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. His body was given; His blood was shed. By His grace, we who were far off have been brought near.  


And now, having been made new in Christ, we no longer belong to this passing world. We are called to live by faith, to endure faithfully, and to follow our rejected yet exalted Savior. In that sense, Hebrews says of God’s faithful people: “the world was not worthy of them.”  


Therefore, let us receive the bread and the cup with deep humility: not boasting in ourselves, but rejoicing in Christ. Once unworthy sinners; now beloved children of God. Once living for this world; now belonging to the kingdom that cannot be shaken.
