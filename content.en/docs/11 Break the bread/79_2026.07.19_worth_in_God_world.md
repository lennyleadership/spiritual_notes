# message
Communion Meditation: Unworthy, Yet Chosen  
Brothers and sisters, as we come to the Lord’s table, we remember that none of us comes here because we are worthy in ourselves.

John the Baptist said of Jesus, “I am not worthy” even to perform the lowest service for Him. Paul said, “I am the least of the apostles, unworthy to be called an apostle, because I persecuted the church of God” (1 Corinthians 15:9). Paul did not forget his past; yet he also said, “By the grace of God I am what I am” (1 Corinthians 15:10).

That is the wonder of the table before us.

We do not come because we have lived a perfect week.  
We do not come because we have earned God’s acceptance.  
We do not come because our service makes us deserving.

We come because Christ’s body was given for us and His blood was shed for us.

At the cross, Jesus took people who were far from God and brought them near. He took the guilty and offered forgiveness. He took those who were unworthy and, by grace, made them His own people.

Hebrews says concerning the faithful servants of God that the world was not worthy of them (Hebrews 11:38). This does not make us proud. It makes us grateful. In ourselves, we were unworthy; but in Christ, we have been chosen, forgiven, sanctified, and called to belong to God.

So, as we take the bread, let us remember His body—given not because we deserved it, but because He loved us.

As we take the cup, let us remember the new covenant in His blood—blood that cleanses our conscience, forgives our sin, and makes us God’s people.

Let us examine ourselves honestly. Let us repent where repentance is needed. Let us forgive one another. And let us receive these emblems with humble faith and joyful thanksgiving.

Prayer for the bread:  
Father, we thank You for the body of Your Son, Jesus Christ, given for us. We confess that we are not worthy in ourselves, yet we praise You that Your grace has brought us near. Help us to remember His sacrifice with reverence, gratitude, and faith. In Jesus’ name, amen.

Prayer for the cup:  
Father, we thank You for the blood of Jesus, shed for the forgiveness of our sins. Thank You for the new covenant, by which You have made us Your people and called us to serve You. May we live humbly, faithfully, and gratefully until He comes again. In Jesus’ name, amen. 

<div class = "quote">
Once we were unworthy sinners before a holy God. But Christ died for us, redeemed us, and made us His own. Now, as those who live by faith and belong to Christ, we are people whom the world is not worthy of.
</div>
